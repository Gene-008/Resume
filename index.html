researchExperience

Reduced Dynamic Visual Capture in Monocular Viewing (2015)

Binocular viewing requires disparity to provide accurate depth perception. A baseball thrown from person A to person B is an object that looms towards person B and recedes from person A; correspondingly, the auditory effects the ball makes as it travels increases to person B and decreases away from person A. Here, these conditions are in congruency

In the experimental condition, incongruency occurs when an object looms (recedes) yet auditorily decreases (increases) representing an unnatural relationship in how our human auditory-visual systems behave. Here, we hypothesized that in individuals with monocular viewing (that is, one eye closed or surgically removed) would have a harder time accurately discriminating between the direction of the baseball and asked to accurately provide the modal selection.

We found no significant statistical evidence to reject our null hypothesis. This is encouraging because it highlights how adaptive our biological A/V systems are.
